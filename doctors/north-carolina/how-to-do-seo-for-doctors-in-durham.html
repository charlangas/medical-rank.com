8 Ways to Make Your Website a Patient-Attracting Machine

  1. Target Local Keywords: Think like your patients. What are they typing into Google at 2 AM?
    • "Best Doctor near me in Durham"
    • "Emergency medical care near me"
    • "Best Doctor in Durham"
  2. Build Location-Specific Pages: Covering various locations? Dedicate a page to each area. Spread the medical love across Durham!
  3. Pimp Your 'About Us' and 'Contact' Pages: Keep your NAP (Name, Address, Phone) consistent. Throw in a Google Map for good measure.
  4. Show Some Local Flavor: Create content focused on Durham's health scene. Participate in local happenings. Showcase your Durham-based team.
  5. Reviews Are Everything: Encourage happy patients to spread the love online. And always respond - good or bad. (Yes, even to that one patient who complains about everything.)
  6. Build Local Street Cred: Ensure your practice appears in Durham directories and medical listings. Collaborate with nearby establishments. Become Durham's go-to Doctor!
  7. Tech It Up: Make sure your site is mobile-friendly, speedy, and easy for search engines to understand. No one likes a slow, clunky website - especially not Google.
  8. Blog Like a Local: Create a blog addressing Durham-specific medical concerns. Become the trusted voice for local medical matters.

For many patient in Durham, this is their initial encounter with your practice. Time to make it shine!

10 Ways to Make Your Google Business Profile Shine

  1. Secure Your Profile: Take ownership of your Google Business Profile. It's like planting your flag in the digital landscape!
  2. Fill It All In: Don't leave any field empty. Every bit of info helps patients choose you.
  3. Be Precise with Categories: Choose the primary category that truly reflects your medical specialization.
  4. Picture Perfect: Add high-quality photos of your office, team, and equipment. (No, your vacation selfies don't count.)
  5. Post It Up: Keep your profile fresh with regular updates and announcements.
  6. Embrace Feedback: Solicit reviews and address each one. That's right, every single one, including the not-so-happy campers.
  7. Q&A Like a Boss: Answer questions before they're even asked. Be a mind reader!
  8. Maintain Accuracy: Regularly review and update your information. Change is the only constant, after all.
  9. Message Me Maybe: Enable messaging if it's available in your area. Be there when patients need you.
  10. Analyze and Adapt: Leverage Google's Insights to track performance and refine your approach.
